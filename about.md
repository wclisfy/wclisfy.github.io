<h1>About</h1>

<p>
  I'm <strong>Willis Chun Lai Wong</strong>, a linguist currently pursuing an
  MPhil in Linguistics at <strong>The Chinese University of Hong Kong</strong>,
  where I also received my BA in Linguistics (First Class).
</p>

<p>
  My research asks how the computational system of language handles
  <strong>structural symmetry</strong>. Merge can freely combine two roots
  that are featurally indistinguishable; deterministic interface evaluators
  then face a choiceless computation problem — they cannot arbitrarily target
  one element over its twin. I formalize this as the <em>Choiceless Computation
  Theorem</em> and use it to derive a unified typology spanning Sinitic AABB /
  ABAB reduplication, ATB extraction, and Right Node Raising.
</p>

<h2 class="section-heading">Research interests</h2>

<ul>
  <li>Syntactic symmetry; automorphism-based labeling; crash-tolerant syntax</li>
  <li>The Merge–interface boundary: Cyclic Linearization, PF deletion, interface conditions</li>
  <li>Coordination, ellipsis, Right Node Raising, ATB extraction</li>
  <li>Distributed Morphology &amp; Sinitic reduplication (Cantonese, Mandarin)</li>
  <li>Prosody–morphology interface: syllable fusion, ideophones</li>
</ul>
