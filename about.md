<h1>About</h1>

<p>
  I'm <strong>Willis Chun Lai Wong</strong>, a linguist currently pursuing an
  MPhil in Linguistics at <strong>The Chinese University of Hong Kong</strong>
  (expected July 2026), where I also received my BA in Linguistics (First
  Class Honours). My MPhil thesis is
  <em>The Architecture of Reduplication: Structural (A)symmetries in Cantonese
  Adjectives</em>.
</p>

<p>
  My research asks how the computational system of language handles
  <strong>structural symmetry</strong>. Merge can freely combine two roots
  that are featurally indistinguishable; deterministic interface evaluators
  then face a choiceless computation problem — they cannot arbitrarily target
  one element over its twin. I formalize this as the <em>Choiceless
  Computation Theorem</em> and use it to derive a unified typology spanning
  Sinitic AABB / ABAB reduplication, ATB extraction, and Right Node Raising.
</p>

<h2 class="section-heading">Research interests</h2>

<p>
  <strong>Syntax–morphology interface.</strong>
  Distributed Morphology; Minimalism; reduplication; roots; labelling algorithm.
</p>

<p>
  <strong>Morphology–prosody interface.</strong>
  Iconicity; syllable fusion; prosodic structure.
</p>
