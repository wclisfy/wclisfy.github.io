About

I'm Willis Chun Lai Wong, a linguist currently pursuing an MPhil in Linguistics at The Chinese University of Hong Kong (expected July 2026), where I also received my BA in Linguistics (First Class Honours). My MPhil thesis is The Architecture of Reduplication: Structural (A)symmetries in Cantonese Adjectives.

My research asks how the computational system of language handles structural symmetry. Merge can freely combine two roots that are featurally indistinguishable; deterministic interface evaluators then face a choiceless computation problem — they cannot arbitrarily target one element over its twin. I formalize this as the Choiceless Computation Theorem and use it to derive a unified typology spanning Sinitic AABB / ABAB reduplication, ATB extraction, and Right Node Raising.

Research interests

Syntax–morphology interface. Distributed Morphology; Minimalism; reduplication; roots; labelling algorithm.

Morphology–prosody interface. Iconicity; syllable fusion; prosodic structure.
